HidHide

Hiding Controllers from SteamInput

The guide is made to hide controllers, here Joycon Charging Grip, from SteamInput, normally cloaking it to play with in games, but if you prefer to use a third party program, you need to hide it from SteamInput, otherwise it's totally out of controls, like disabling gyroscope and accelerometer, even every buttons and sticks if you forcing. Let's see...

Adding Steam and Steam Web Helper to the List of Applications

The first step is to add to the list Steam.exe and SteamWebHelper.exe to HidHide, and check the box: inverse application cloak, to prevent Steam to see the controller. You need also to remove HidHide from the list, it's added by default. But HidHide need to see your controller by letting this guy cloaking your controller, so remove HidHide.exe from the list of applications.

Enable Hiding to Added Applications

The second step is to connect your controller, and check the box where it appear in device list to lock it from added applications. Check the box: enable device hiding. You need to reconnect your controller for it take effect.

Now It Go Well

The third and last step is to launch Steam to see everything is perfect now. You can use your third party program to play with your controller as a virtual Xbox controller. Steam will only see a Xbox controller. You can navigate into Steam Big Picture with your controller like with a Xbox controller. Repeat the steps when you want to use your other controllers. You can close HidHide, it don't need to be open to hide your controller from Steam, even after restarting your PC. It's almost magic.
